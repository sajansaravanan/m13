NASA's Earendel: The Most Distant Star

Earendel is the most distant star ever detected, located about 12.9 billion light-years away.

Discovered by NASA's James Webb Space Telescope, it reveals new insights into the early universe.

The star's light has traveled for nearly 13 billion years to reach us, giving us a glimpse of the cosmos as it was.

Observations of Earendel help astronomers understand star formation in the early universe.

This discovery highlights Webb's capability to explore the distant universe in unprecedented detail.
